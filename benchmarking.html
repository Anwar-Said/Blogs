Leveraging Graph Neural Networks for Neuroimaging: A benchmarking Perspective

Github Link Link to the paper

We present a curated collection of graph-based neuroimaging datasets derived from the HCP Young Adult dataset , which encompass a diverse range of behavioral and cognitive traits. Our collection incorporates a variety of tasks including activity decomposition, gender and age classification, as well as the prediction of fluid intelligence and working memory. These tasks offer insights into various dimensions of cognitive function and behaviour, making this collection a valuable resource for the exploration of brain activity and neuroimaging research
The datasets are primarily divided into two main categories: Demographic and Behavioral Attributes, and Cognitive Tasks. Each category encapsulates distinct aspects of the collected data and serves unique analytical purposes.


  • Demographic and behavioral attributes:
    The category of demographic and behavioral attributes in our dataset is comprised of gender, age, and various activity tasks. The gender attribute facilitates a binary classification with the categories being male and female. Age is categorized into three distinct groups: early adulthood (22-25 years), prime working age (26-30 years), and mature adulthood (31-35 years). A fourth category for ages 36 and above was eliminated as it contained only 14 subjects, to maintain a balanced dataset. The activity attribute is divided into seven classes, each denoting a different cognitive or behavioral task: Emotion, Gambling, Language, Motor Task, Relational Task, Social Task, and Working Memory Task. Each class represents a unique task domain, offering insights into different facets of human cognition and behavior as assessed by fMRI studies. For demographic and behavioral attribute classification, we introduced six datasets: HCPActivity, HCPGender, HCPAge, DynHCPActivity, DynHCPGender, and DynHCPAge. The first three datasets consist of static representations of brain graphs, while the latter three encompass dynamic graph representations.
  • Cognitive tasks:
    The cognitive traits category of our dataset comprises two significant tasks: Working Memory and Fluid Intelligence. Working memory refers to an individual's capacity to temporarily hold and manipulate information, a crucial aspect that influences higher cognitive functions such as reasoning, comprehension, and learning. Fluid intelligence represents the ability to solve novel problems, independent of any knowledge from the past. It demonstrates the capacity to analyze complex relationships, identify patterns, and derive solutions in dynamic situations. Both these traits, quantified as continuous variables in our dataset, are treated as regression tasks in our analysis. We aim to predict the performance or scores related to these cognitive traits based on the fMRI data, hence providing a richer understanding of the neural correlates of these essential cognitive abilities. We generate four datasets under cognitive traits: HCP Fluid Intelligence (HCPFI), HCP Working Memory (HCPWM), DynHCPFI and DynHCPWM.
