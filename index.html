About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral research scholar at Vanderbilt University, TN. My research belongs to the area of graph representation learning (GRL), an emerging field having far-reaching applications in a diverse range of industries, from drug discovery and optimization to recommendation and forecasting. My research mainly focuses on the design of novel GRL approaches to enhance their performance and apply them to solve complex real-world problems. Working closely with a team of experts, I have developed several GRL approaches that have been applied to problems such as circuit design completion, link prediction in Ethereum data, and fraud detection in social networks. In addition to my work in GRL, I also work in data science, graph theory, and network science. Recently, I have expanded my research to include GRL applications in the field of electronic design automation as well as neuroscience. Through my research, I strive to make meaningful contributions to the field of GRL and improve our understanding of the complex systems that shape our world.
